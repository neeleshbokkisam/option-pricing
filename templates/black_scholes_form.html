Black-Scholes Option Pricing

The Black-Scholes model is a mathematical model for pricing an options contract. The model calculates the theoretical price of European put and call options, ignoring any dividends paid during the option's lifetime. It assumes the market is efficient, meaning it reflects all relevant information, and the risk-free rate and volatility of the underlying asset are constant.
